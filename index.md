chemical engineering to modern data science and machine learning, with a strong emphasis on
hands-on programming in Python and applications to real chemical and engineering datasets.

## Course Logistics (Snapshot)

- **Prerequisites:** MATH 225, 227, 257, or 415.
- **Restrictions:** Junior or Senior standing (for undergraduates).  
  Knowledge of essential programming constructs (functions, loops, conditionals) in some
  language (C/C++, Fortran, Java, etc.) is required. Basic proficiency in Python is strongly
  recommended.
- **Course website for submissions:** [canvas.illinois.edu](https://canvas.illinois.edu)

### Grading Breakdown

- Homework (10 Jupyter notebook assignments): **40%**
- Exam 1 (take-home, 48 hours): **15%**
- Exam 2 (take-home, 48 hours): **15%**
- Final Project: **30%**
  - Written report and GitHub code: **20%**
  - In-class presentation: **10%**

Grades are curved with a median in the B/B+ range. The curve can only help you:
- 90% is at least an A-
- 80% is at least a B-
- 70% is at least a C-
- 60% is at least a D-
